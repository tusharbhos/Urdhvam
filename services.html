Bore Charger Technology

Groundwater Aquifer Mapping

Groundwater Aquifer Mapping
Groundwater aquifer mapping is the process of identifying, characterizing, and visualizing underground layers of water-bearing rock or sediment (called aquifers) that store and transmit groundwater. This mapping is crucial for sustainable water resource management, particularly in regions that rely heavily on groundwater for drinking, agriculture, and industry. GIS-based aquifer visualization Watershed planning and implementation

Rooftop Rainwater Harvesting (RWH)

Rooftop Rainwater Harvesting (RWH)
is a sustainable method of collecting and storing rainwater that falls on the roofs of buildings for later use. It is one of the simplest and most effective ways to conserve water, particularly in urban and semi-urban areas where water demand is high and groundwater levels may be depleting.
  • Customized solutions for homes, schools, industries
  • Includes design and implementation

Groundwater Pollution Studies

Groundwater Pollution Studies
is the contamination of underground water sources (aquifers) by harmful substances. These pollutants seep through the soil and rock layers, often due to human activities, and degrade the quality of groundwater, making it unsafe for drinking, irrigation, and industrial use. – Analysis of industrial, sea water intrusion, arsenic/fluoride issues
